Trade Simulator
How to use the Trade Simulator Plan trades, calculate risk and log results to your journal
1
Enter market & direction
Type the instrument (e.g. EUR/USD, BTC/USD, QQQ) and choose LONG if you expect the price to rise, or SHORT if you expect it to fall.
2
Select the market type
Pick Forex for standard pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD), Forex JPY for yen pairs (USD/JPY), or Index / Commodity for indices, gold, ETFs and crypto.
3
Set entry, stop loss & take profit
Enter your planned levels. The metrics panel updates instantly — showing R:R ratio, pip distance and exact dollar risk and reward.
4
Configure account & risk %
Enter your account balance and the percentage you're prepared to risk. 1–2% per trade is the professional standard. The simulator calculates your position size automatically.
5
Add reasoning (optional)
Document your entry reason (pattern, level, confluence) and exit reason after closing. This builds a reviewable trade log that helps identify what works.
6
Log to your journal
Click Win (TP Hit) or Loss (SL Hit) to record a closed trade, or Log as Open to track a live position with real-time P&L monitoring.
Metrics explained
R:R RatioHow much you stand to gain vs. lose. 1:2 means risking $1 to make $2. Aim for at least 1:1.5 on every trade.
Risk ($)Exact dollar amount lost if stop loss is hit. Calculated as account balance × risk %.
Reward ($)Exact dollar amount gained if take profit is hit. Reward ÷ Risk = R:R ratio.
Min Win RateThe minimum win rate to break even at your R:R. At 1:3 you only need 25% winners to be profitable long-term.
Market TypeForex standard ÷ 0.0001 per pip · JPY pairs ÷ 0.01 per pip · Index/Commodity uses raw points (1 pt = 1 unit).
Open TradeLogs the trade as active. The journal will show live P&L updates if a price feed is connected. Close it later via the journal.

Auto Signals — Daily Market Analysis
How to use Auto Signals Connect a data source, scan markets and act on high-confidence setups
1
Connect a data source
Select Twelve Data or Alpha Vantage, paste your free API key and click Connect. Or choose Demo to explore simulated signals with no key required.
2
Choose a timeframe
Use Daily for swing trading signals (1–5 day holds). Use 4H for shorter intraday setups that resolve within hours to 2 days.
3
Run the market scan
Click Scan Markets. Sharpe analyses all monitored instruments using EMA trend alignment, RSI momentum, ATR-based SL/TP and price action pattern recognition.
4
Review signal cards
Each card shows the instrument, direction (LONG/SHORT), confidence %, entry zone, stop loss, take profit, R:R ratio and the specific pattern that triggered the signal.
5
Enable auto-log
Tick Auto-log signals ≥ X% to automatically save qualifying signals to your Trade Journal. Set the threshold to 75%+ to avoid low-quality noise.
6
Auto-execute via OANDA
Connect your OANDA API token and account ID to place orders automatically when signals fire. Start on a practice account before switching to live trading.

3. General Market Risk

Financial markets are inherently unpredictable. Prices of instruments including foreign exchange, commodities, equities, and cryptocurrencies can fall as well as rise rapidly and without warning. 4. Leverage Risk

Many trading instruments are available on margin or with leverage, which amplifies both profits and losses. A small adverse price movement can result in losses greater than your initial deposit. You should fully understand how leverage works and whether it is appropriate for your circumstances before trading.

5. Cryptocurrency Risk

Cryptocurrencies are highly volatile and largely unregulated. They are not backed by any government or central bank. Crypto markets operate 24/7 and can experience extreme price swings in short periods. Regulatory changes could significantly affect the value or legality of specific cryptocurrencies.
