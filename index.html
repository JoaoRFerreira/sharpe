Signals & Scanner
The scanner runs every 4 hours via GitHub Actions, analysing price data using EMA crossovers, RSI, ATR, candlestick patterns, ICT order blocks, and supply/demand zones. Critically, an EMA50 trend gate blocks counter-trend signals before they reach you — if a bearish pattern appears while price is above EMA50, no SHORT signal fires. Each signal includes direction, entry zone, stop loss, two take-profit targets, confidence score, and a reason why it fired.
The EMA50 trend gate is a filter that blocks counter-trend signals. LONG signals only fire when price is above the 50-period EMA (bullish bias). SHORT signals only fire when price is below it. Without this filter, the scanner would generate bearish patterns during uptrends — which is exactly what caused two consecutive EUR/CHF losses before the gate was added. You can learn more in Learning → Trend Filters.
Each signal card shows a market regime badge: Trending ↑ (EMA and RSI both confirm bullish direction), Trending ↓ (bearish), Overbought (RSI ≥ 68), Oversold (RSI ≤ 32), or Ranging (no clear bias). This gives you the market context for each signal so you can assess whether conditions support the trade beyond the pattern alone.
When the scanner produces no signal for an instrument, the Signals page shows a collapsed "Why Not Now" panel below the market strip. It explains the absence: "RSI at 54 — not at an extreme. Wait for RSI > 68." or "Ranging structure — no trend to trade against." Silence from the scanner is now informative rather than just empty.
When the same signal direction fires on more than one timeframe simultaneously (e.g. Daily and 4H both show LONG for EUR/USD), the signal card shows a confluence badge. A gold ⚡ Triple TF Confluence badge means all three timeframes agree — this is the highest-confidence scenario the scanner produces. Multi-Timeframe Confluence is available on the Pro plan.
Signal cards show the scanner's historical win rate for that specific pattern (e.g. "Three Black Crows · 14 signals · 58% win rate · avg +32 pips"). This is calculated from all closed paper trades with a minimum of 3 data points. It lets you see which patterns have the strongest track record in real market conditions, not just backtests.
The Signals page shows the week's upcoming high-impact economic events (NFP, FOMC, CPI, ECB decisions) filtered to USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CHF, AUD, and CAD. Events happening today are highlighted. This helps you avoid opening new paper trades immediately before major data releases that can invalidate technical setups. Requires a Twelve Data API key in Settings.
Paper Trading & Positions
Paper trading simulates real trades with no money at risk. Click Paper Trade on any signal to queue a pending entry. A price monitor checks every 30 seconds while the app is open and every 1 minute server-side when it's closed. When price enters the entry zone, the position opens automatically. When stop loss or take profit is hit, it closes automatically and logs the result to your track record.
Auto Paper Trading automatically queues a pending entry for every signal above your confidence threshold — no clicking Paper Trade required. Go to Settings → Auto Paper Trade, enable the toggle, and set your minimum confidence (e.g. 70%). The system handles everything server-side via the signal scanner's 4-hour cycle. Requires Trader or Pro plan.
When you have two or more open positions in the same direction on correlated instruments (e.g. Long EUR/USD and Long GBP/USD), the Positions page shows an amber warning: "EUR/USD and GBP/USD are 84% correlated — you are effectively 2× long USD weakness." Correlation is computed from the last 20 daily closes. This is only relevant if you have multiple open positions in the same direction.
Every closed position has a Replay ▶ button. Clicking it opens a full-screen dark chart and plays back the candle sequence from your entry to your close — bar by bar, with entry, stop loss, take profit, and exit price lines overlaid. Speed is adjustable from 0.5× to 8×. Use it to understand exactly what the market did during your trade and whether your decision to close was correct.
Journal & Analytics
The Trade Journal lets you log any trade — manual or from a signal — with entry price, stop loss, take profit, entry reasoning, exit reasoning, and tags. Every closed trade is automatically graded A–F based on execution quality: whether you hit your target, how your result compares to your average win/loss, and whether you documented your reasoning. You can filter by market, status, or grade, and export to CSV.
Every closed trade receives an automatic letter grade. A = win above 70% of your average win, with entry and exit reasoning documented. B = any win with notes. C = a disciplined small loss (under 80% of your average loss). D = a loss at normal size. F = a loss more than 2× your average loss — indicates a stop-loss violation or early exit from a winning trade. Grades appear as badges in the journal and can be filtered.
The Sharpe Score is a single 0–100 composite performance metric shown on your Dashboard. It combines: Win Rate (up to 35 pts), Profit Factor (up to 30 pts), Max Drawdown (up to 20 pts), and Sample Size (up to 15 pts). Labels: 0–25 Developing, 26–45 Building, 46–60 Solid, 61–75 Strong, 76–100 Elite. It requires at least 5 closed trades to calculate.
Profit Factor is gross winning pips divided by gross losing pips. Above 1.0 = net-profitable. Above 1.5 = solid edge. Above 2.0 = strong. It matters because it captures both win rate and the size of wins versus losses together — a system that wins 40% of the time but wins 3× more than it loses can have a higher Profit Factor than one that wins 70% at 1:1 R:R.
The Backtester replays the signal strategy on historical candle data already stored in the database (seeded via Admin → Seed Candles). Select an instrument and timeframe, run the backtest, and see the simulated equity curve, win rate, and profit factor as if the signals had been followed over the historical period. This lets you validate a strategy before trusting live signals.
