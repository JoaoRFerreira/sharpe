Trade Simulator
How to use the Trade Simulator Plan trades, calculate risk and log results to your journal
1
Enter market & direction
Type the instrument (e.g. EUR/USD, BTC/USD, QQQ) and choose LONG if you expect the price to rise, or SHORT if you expect it to fall.
2
Select the market type
Pick Forex for standard pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD), Forex JPY for yen pairs (USD/JPY), or Index / Commodity for indices, gold, ETFs and crypto.
3
Set entry, stop loss & take profit
Enter your planned levels. The metrics panel updates instantly — showing R:R ratio, pip distance and exact dollar risk and reward.
4
Configure account & risk %
Enter your account balance and the percentage you're prepared to risk. 1–2% per trade is the professional standard. The simulator calculates your position size automatically.
5
Add reasoning (optional)
Document your entry reason (pattern, level, confluence) and exit reason after closing. This builds a reviewable trade log that helps identify what works.
6
Log to your journal
Click Win (TP Hit) or Loss (SL Hit) to record a closed trade, or Log as Open to track a live position with real-time P&L monitoring.
Metrics explained
R:R RatioHow much you stand to gain vs. lose. 1:2 means risking $1 to make $2. Aim for at least 1:1.5 on every trade.
Risk ($)Exact dollar amount lost if stop loss is hit. Calculated as account balance × risk %.
Reward ($)Exact dollar amount gained if take profit is hit. Reward ÷ Risk = R:R ratio.
Min Win RateThe minimum win rate to break even at your R:R. At 1:3 you only need 25% winners to be profitable long-term.
Market TypeForex standard ÷ 0.0001 per pip · JPY pairs ÷ 0.01 per pip · Index/Commodity uses raw points (1 pt = 1 unit).
Open TradeLogs the trade as active. The journal will show live P&L updates if a price feed is connected. Close it later via the journal.

Auto Signals — Daily Market Analysis
How to use Auto Signals Understand timeframes, confidence scores, and how to act on signals
1
Choose a timeframe
Use Daily for swing setups (1–5 day holds). 4H for intraday setups resolving within hours to 2 days. 1W (Pro) for weekly position trades. All signals are pre-filtered by the EMA50 trend gate — counter-trend patterns are blocked before reaching you.
2
Read the signal card context
Each card shows confidence %, entry/SL/TP, pattern name, market regime (Trending ↑/↓, Ranging, Overbought/Oversold), and the scanner's historical win rate for that pattern. Pro users also see multi-timeframe confluence badges when Daily and 4H agree.
3
Check "Why Not Now" for silent instruments
Instruments with no signal are listed below the market strip with a reason — "RSI at 54, not yet extreme enough" or "Ranging structure — no trend to trade." Silence is now information, not absence of it.
4
Act: Paper Trade, journal, or enable auto-log
Click Paper Trade to queue a position. Use Auto-log to save qualifying signals to your journal automatically. Enable Auto Paper Trading in Settings to have the system open and close positions server-side — even when your browser is closed.
Signal card reference
Confidence 65–74%Partial alignment. Treat as a watchlist candidate — wait for additional confirmation.
Confidence 75–84%Solid signal. Most key factors aligned. Standard actionable threshold for most traders.
Confidence 85–93%High-conviction setup. All major factors confirm — EMA50, price structure, RSI, volume. Rare — treat as priority.
⚡ Triple TF ConfluenceThe same direction fired on Daily, 4H, and Weekly. Statistically the highest-probability setup the scanner produces. Pro only.
Pattern win rateThe scanner's historical win rate for this specific pattern across all closed paper trades. Minimum 3 data points before shown.
Market Regime badgeTrending ↑/↓ = EMA and RSI both confirm direction. Ranging = no clear trend. Overbought/Oversold = RSI at extreme. Context for the signal's environment.

How Paper Trading works
1Click Paper Trade on any signal — the order is queued as Pending at the signal's entry price, with the predefined stop loss and take profit. Enable Auto Paper Trading in Settings to skip this step entirely.
2Prices are monitored every 30 seconds while the app is open, and every 1 minute server-side when it's closed. When the market reaches the entry zone, the order moves to Open automatically.
3Positions close automatically at take profit or stop loss. You can also close manually. A Correlation warning appears if two or more open positions are >70% correlated — you may be taking the same trade twice.
4Every closed trade feeds your Sharpe Score, win rate, and leaderboard ranking. Hit Replay ▶ on any closed position to watch it bar-by-bar and review your decision. No real money is ever involved.

How Paper Trading works
